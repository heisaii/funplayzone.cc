# World Cup Quarterfinal Watch Guide: Spain-Belgium, Norway-England, Argentina-Switzerland

France have already booked the first semifinal place at the 2026 World Cup. The next three quarterfinals will decide whether the tournament becomes a favorite's bracket, a superstar bracket, or a full late-stage surprise.

The remaining schedule is straightforward in date order: Spain vs Belgium on July 10, Norway vs England on July 11, and Argentina vs Switzerland later on July 11. For viewers in Asia, those matches appear across July 11 and July 12 local listings, which is why schedules can look slightly different depending on where they are published.

Here is what each match is really about.

## Spain vs Belgium: control against survival instinct

Spain enter the Belgium match with the cleaner tournament profile. They have controlled long stretches, protected their defensive structure, and looked like one of the teams most capable of making the ball itself feel like a defensive weapon. If they beat Belgium, they get France in a semifinal that could decide the title mood of the entire tournament.

Belgium are harder to read. Their 4-1 Round of 16 win over the United States gave them momentum, but their tournament has also carried questions around Kevin De Bruyne's rhythm, Romelu Lukaku's role, and whether this group can survive long spells without the ball against an elite possession team.

The goalkeeper matchup is a serious subplot. Spain's Unai Simon has been central to their build-up and clean-sheet run, while Belgium's Thibaut Courtois remains one of the few keepers who can keep a team alive through pressure alone. If Spain dominate territory but cannot score early, Courtois becomes more than a shot-stopper. He becomes Belgium's way of staying in the match long enough for one chance to matter.

Key question: can Belgium turn Spain's control into frustration before Spain turn it into a goal?

## Norway vs England: Haaland against the England machine

Norway vs England is the quarterfinal with the clearest headline. Erling Haaland has already helped Norway remove Brazil. England arrive with Harry Kane, Jude Bellingham, and the confidence of a chaotic 3-2 win over Mexico.

The tactical tension is simple but severe. England cannot let the match become a delivery system for Haaland. They need to control Norway's wide service, defend second balls, and avoid the kind of open transitional game that makes Haaland feel inevitable. Norway, meanwhile, do not need to dominate possession. They need to make their attacks heavy, direct, and psychologically uncomfortable.

England's right side is worth watching because Jarell Quansah's suspension has created a selection problem. In a normal match, a defensive adjustment is a detail. Against Norway, it may be the route Norway try to attack for 90 minutes.

Key question: can England control the match without becoming cautious enough to invite Haaland's best moments?

## Argentina vs Switzerland: champions against the tournament spoiler

Argentina's comeback win over Egypt was one of the tournament's most dramatic recent results. Down 2-0 late, they found goals through Cristian Romero, Lionel Messi, and Enzo Fernandez to stay alive. That kind of comeback can do two opposite things: it can drain a team, or it can convince a team that the tournament is bending toward it.

Switzerland are the kind of opponent that can punish any emotional hangover. They are organized, patient, and comfortable making a favorite solve a long defensive puzzle. They do not need the ball to feel in control of a match's rhythm. They need compactness, discipline, and enough quality in transition or set pieces to make Argentina nervous.

For Argentina, the match is about avoiding panic. Messi does not need to dominate every minute. Argentina need to make sure the game stays close enough for his influence to matter late, without asking for another emergency comeback.

Key question: do Argentina control Switzerland early, or does the match become a 75-minute test of champion nerve?

## What the semifinals could look like

France will face Spain or Belgium. The other semifinal will come from Norway, England, Argentina, and Switzerland.

The cleanest favorite path would be France vs Spain and Argentina vs England. The most explosive star path would be France vs Belgium and Norway vs Argentina, with Mbappe, Lukaku, Haaland, and Messi carrying the marketing department's dream. The biggest spoiler route would involve Switzerland extending their run and forcing everyone to talk less about names and more about structure.

That is why these three matches matter beyond their own scorelines. They decide the style of the final week.

## How to watch the rest of the round

For Spain vs Belgium, watch the first 20 minutes of Belgium's defensive block. If they are pulled apart early, Spain may control the night. If Belgium stay compact and Courtois settles in, the match can grow tense.

For Norway vs England, watch the spaces behind England's fullbacks and the way Norway enter wide crossing positions. If Haaland is receiving service facing goal, England have a problem. If he is mostly wrestling with center backs for hopeful balls, England are doing their job.

For Argentina vs Switzerland, watch the score at halftime. A level match helps Switzerland believe. An early Argentina goal forces Switzerland into a different game and gives Messi more room later.

The quarterfinals are not only asking who is better. They are asking which kind of team the tournament wants to reward: control, star power, survival, or structure.
