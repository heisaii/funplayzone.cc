# Who Will Win the 2026 World Cup Now? France Lead, but Spain and Argentina Still Complicate the Bracket

As of July 10, 2026, the 2026 World Cup has reached the point where a prediction finally has to be specific. The quarterfinals are underway, France have already beaten Morocco 2-0, and the rest of the last-eight bracket still has three unresolved doors: Spain vs Belgium on July 10, then Norway vs England and Argentina vs Switzerland on July 11.

That timing matters. This is not a full semifinal preview yet. It is a title forecast made in the middle of the quarterfinals, after the first major favorite has done its job but before Spain, Argentina, England, Norway, Belgium, and Switzerland have had their final say.

My pick right now: France are the narrow favorites to win the tournament. Spain are the best alternative. Argentina are the emotional danger. England and Norway are live outsiders because their route contains match-winning forwards. Belgium and Switzerland are longer shots, but neither should be treated as decorative at this stage.

## Why France are the pick

France have already crossed the line that everyone else in the bracket still has to cross. That is the simplest advantage, and it is not a small one. A quarterfinal win removes 90 minutes of risk, possible extra time, possible penalties, injury exposure, and the mental drag of waiting.

The 2-0 win over Morocco also fit the profile of a champion rather than just a contender. France did not need a chaotic shootout. They had to solve a stubborn opponent, survive a first half that did not turn cleanly in their favor, and then let elite attackers decide the second half. Kylian Mbappe and Ousmane Dembele give Didier Deschamps exactly what knockout football rewards: speed, invention, and the ability to make a tight match feel unfair very quickly.

France also have tournament memory. That phrase can be overused, but it matters here. They know what a semifinal week feels like. They know how to manage a match that is not pretty. They know how to win without looking permanently fluent. In the final stages of a World Cup, that is often more useful than playing the most attractive football for 60 minutes.

## The Spain problem

The biggest argument against a France title is not fatigue, pressure, or history. It is Spain.

If Spain beat Belgium, they become France's semifinal opponent, and that matchup may be the technical final hidden inside the bracket. Spain's tournament has been built on control. They can keep the ball, compress the pitch, protect their goalkeeper, and make opponents chase for long spells. They also have enough young wide talent to avoid becoming possession for possession's sake.

That is why Spain are not simply "second favorites" in a lazy ranking. They are the team with the clearest tactical answer to France. If France want transitions, Spain can deny them. If France want to isolate fullbacks, Spain can overload around the ball. If France want the game to become emotional, Spain can make it administrative.

But Spain still have to beat Belgium first. That is why France remain ahead in this forecast. One team is already in the semifinals. The other still has to handle Thibaut Courtois, Romelu Lukaku, Kevin De Bruyne, and the tension of a quarterfinal at SoFi Stadium.

## Argentina are dangerous for a different reason

Argentina do not have to be the cleanest team left to be terrifying. They have Lionel Messi, they have the muscle memory of winning, and they have just survived the kind of Round of 16 comeback against Egypt that can either exhaust a team or convince it that destiny is still involved.

That makes Argentina a strange forecast. On balance, they look less stable than France or Spain. They have allowed a match to run away from them. They have had to summon emergency football. They are also old enough in key places for every high-tempo game to carry a cost.

Yet Argentina are built for moments. If they get past Switzerland, they will not need to be perfect to trouble England or Norway. They will need one spell of control, one Messi pass, one set piece, or one late sequence where everyone else tightens up and their champions do not.

## England or Norway could break the prediction

The Norway vs England quarterfinal is the bracket's loudest physical question. England have Harry Kane and Jude Bellingham. Norway have Erling Haaland and a team that has already removed Brazil. That is not a normal underdog resume.

England's case is balance and depth, though Jarell Quansah's suspension leaves Thomas Tuchel with an awkward defensive adjustment. Norway's case is more direct: they have the most frightening penalty-box striker in the world and a route to the final that no longer includes Brazil.

If either team reaches the final, the match can become about one or two decisive chances rather than a long argument about control. France and Spain would both be favored in that scenario, but not by enough to make the final feel predictable.

## The current title order

If forced to rank the remaining teams on July 10, before Spain vs Belgium kicks off, my order is:

- France
- Spain
- Argentina
- England
- Norway
- Belgium
- Switzerland

France top the list because they combine proven knockout habits with the immediate advantage of already being through. Spain are close because their style can make even France uncomfortable. Argentina sit third because champions with Messi do not need a perfect path to create a perfect night.

The winner is most likely to come from France or Spain. The final could still belong to Argentina, England, or Norway if the bracket turns on a superstar moment. That is the beauty of this stage: the tournament is narrow enough to predict, but still volatile enough to punish anyone who sounds too certain.

## Prediction

France to win the World Cup, with Spain as the team most capable of stopping them.

That forecast may change after Spain vs Belgium, because a Spanish win would set up the direct test. For now, France have the cleanest blend of survival, star power, and tournament control. In July, that is usually the profile that lasts longest.
